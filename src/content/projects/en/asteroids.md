## What it is

A version of the classic **Asteroids**: a ship that moves and shoots, and
asteroids that break into smaller pieces each time they're hit. I built it as a
guided project from the **Boot.dev** course.

## What I learned

- **Object-oriented programming for real**: modeling the ship, the asteroids, and
  the shots as classes, each with its own state and behavior.
- **The "game loop"**: updating positions, detecting collisions, and drawing the
  scene many times per second.
- Using **sprite groups** in Pygame to manage many objects at once without losing
  control of the code.
- **Collision detection** and vector math for movement.

## Why I include it

Even though it came from a course, it was the project where object-oriented
programming really "clicked" for me: going from understanding the theory to
seeing, in something that works, why it's worth splitting a program into objects
that collaborate with each other.
